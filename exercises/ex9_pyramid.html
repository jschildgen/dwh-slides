Exercise 1: Connecting to Pyramid Analytics

  1. Connect to the OTH Exasol database with your FIRSTNAME_LASTNAME user and you find your username and password for Pyramid here:
    select * from common.pyramid_password;
  2. If you worked on your local Exasol database so far, use CREATE TABLE commands to create your target data warehouse schema in your OTH Exasol database and export your data to there by executing the following commands on your local Exasol DB. Make sure that VPN to the OTH is on and that your Exasol VM has internet access. You can configure NAT in the VM settings under Network, and add a port forwarding from host port 8563 to guest port 8563. Then connect your DBeaver to localhost.
    create or replace connection jdbc_con to 'jdbc:exa:194.95.108.54' 
user 'FIRSTNAME_LASTNAME' identified by 'Your OTH Exasol DB Password';

EXPORT myschema.mytable INTO JDBC AT jdbc_con TABLE FIRSTNAME_LASTNAME.mytable;
  3. Open the OTH Pyramid instance in your web browser: http://10.127.0.26:8181 and log in with your Pyramid username and password.
  4. Create a connection from Pyramid to Exasol: Model (yellow DB symbol on the very left) / Direct Model / Exasol / 194.95.108.54, your FIRSTNAME_LASTNAME user, password, schema. Then choose your DWH tables (not the staging area). You can now stop here and continue with the next exercise, in which you create your model. There, your new Exasol connection can be choosen in the Model Lite wizard.

Exercise 2: Model

Basically, you can create Discoveries directly on your Direct Model from Exercise 1. But if you want to use all features (e.g., Time Intelligence), create a new Model Lite.

  • Create a Model Lite: Target: In-Memory, new Database. Then choose your Exasol connection, your schema, and your tables. Select all important columns, check their role (dimension, measure), check foreign keys between your tables.

Exercise 3: Discover, Present, Publish

Create a presentation and / or poster that shows the results of your project: interesting facts and findings; do not focus on how you did it.

  1. Use Discover to create charts.
  2. If you present your results on July 5 or 7, prepare a 5-minute presentation using Pyramid's Present module. Please write your title and your name on the first slide.
  3. If you present your results on July 8 (Open-University Day), create a poster using Pyramid's Publish module. Choose Landscape or Portrait format, A3 (actually, we will print it on A2 format). Please write your title and your name on the poster.
  4. Export your presentation / poster as a PDF by clicking on the gray-blue Run button on the top left. For presentations, this will start your presentation. Then, click on the menu button on the top left, and then Print.
  5. Choose your presentation slot in ELO and Upload your presentation and / or poster PDF to ELO before the deadline (July 4, 23:59). We will print your poster for you.
